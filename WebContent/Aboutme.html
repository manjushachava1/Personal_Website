I am a 16 year old Junior at Mass Academy of Math and Science and an avid enthusiast of Coldplay, The Flash, and pizza.

That's me and my brother.

I live in Tyngsboro, MA, but because no one knows where that is, it is near Lowell, MA. If you don't know where Lowell is either, then you should probably look it up on Google Maps.


My sending school was Chelmsford High School, which is located in Chelmsford, MA, a town neighboring Tyngsboro. I loved CHS because of the faculty, the sense of community, and of course, my friends. Leaving Chelmsford High was tough because it meant I would have to start over. Losing not only my friends, but the connections I had with many of my teachers made me feel sad. Being the risk taker I was, I wanted to challenge myself and try something new.

The end result was my enrollment into Mass Academy.

I play three instruments; violin, guitar, and piano. I have been playing the violin since I was in 4th grade and I loved it all my life. I was section leader of the violin section during my time in high school orchestra, as well as 1st seat and sometimes soloist for some of the pieces. I participated in Chelmsford Symphony Orchestra, which was an after school club designed for students with a passion for music to play pieces which were far more advanced compared to the regular orchestra/band music.

Piano was an instrument I had a passion for since I was little. I hadn't delved into it until I was about 11 years old, which was when I got my first keyboard. I took lessons for more than a year and a half before stopping, for the reason that I wasn't able to allocate enough time for practice. Now piano has become a past time for me rather than a primary instrument, but I still love playing it to this day.

My interest for guitar acquired from listening to bands like Green Day, and of course, Coldplay. I have always loved the sound of the guitar, but nothing inspired me to learn how to play it more than listening to songs mainly by these two bands. After begging my parents to buy me an acoustic guitar, I taught myself how to play many songs, as well as learn basic guitar chords. I struggle with barre chords, like many of the amateur guitar players, but hopefully I'll master those someday.

In addition to playing instruments, I also enjoy singing. Whenever I have free time (which is almost never), I like to pick up my guitar and sing any song that matches my mood. Freshman year was the first time I joined an actual Chorus; before that, I had been in Orchestra my entire life.

The music teachers at CHS encouraged me to join their chorus. Although the Orchestra and Chorus classes ran during the same times of day, I was able to skip Orchestra on certain days to practice with the chorus. Singing with a group of people I admired made me forget my surroundings, and helped me relax greatly. The songs we practiced challenged my vocals and pushed me to practice harder. Unfortunately, I was not able to continue this wonderful experience in my sophomore year due to scheduling conflicts. However, being a part of something extraordinary truly had an impact on my life.
